We fight the decision of big oil companies to destroy the environment and people’s lives by building pipelines.

The Big Issue

Big oil companies make a lot of money by building pipelines, which damage the environment and affect people living near pipelines. Indigenous communities are often the worst affected, but all kinds of communities along pipelines, big and small, suffer from pipelines. Their drinking water is contaminated from pipelines leaking oil into the groundwater, and residents are bullied into selling their land at joke prices to corporations that build these pipelines. The environment is affected too, with trees being cut down to make way, pollution being encouraged from increased oil flow, and pipelines generally destroying natural habitat along long distances.

For The People

As a way to do our part in preventing more pipelines being built, we founded Black Tears, a not-for profit organization to raise awareness about the environmental and social issues of oil pipelines. We believe that we should have control over our planet and our future, not thrust that control into megacorporations' hands. Though we are just students, but what we're fighting for involves more than just us. Join our petition today, and keep reading to find out why you should.

Our Planet Cries Out

Intuitively, our consumption of oil has a negative effect on our society. This leads to the concept of a negative externality of consumption, which is the result of the negative effects of oil pipelines on society. Right now, chances are that you are an oil consumer. You probably drive a car that runs on gasoline, and use electricity, some of which comes from oil or other fossil fuels. Big oil corporations are the suppliers of your oil, shipping it from where it is extracted to where it gets refined and then delivered for final use. You’re probably not concerned about using oil, because of its many benefits to you. However, it is precisely yours, and everyone else’s, consumption of oil that puts burdens on society, a third party.

Think about it: every time you consume oil, you are polluting our planet’s atmosphere and environment (the natural habitat that you live in) by a small increment. These small increments would not be significant individually, but collectively, they add up with every additional unit consumed. Since everyone, like you, is consuming oil, the effects on our society are amplified drastically. One drop of oil makes no difference, but the trillion drops that we use every day will one day turn our “great blue planet” into a black one. Nature cries out, with black tears. The tears of acid rain, of oil spills, of smog, and of pollution.

Why The Buzz?

This issue affects not only Alberta, but also our country and the US. Albertan oil is the biggest deposit in North America, making pipelines a big part of projected Canada-US trade. Oil is one of the few main causes of unsustainable development in the world. Countries like Saudi Arabia whose economies almost wholly depend on oil, are quickly shifting their economic focus into other sectors, as the projected last drop of oil is the year 2050. You heard that right. By 2050, we will run out of oil. Oil needs to be replaced completely by clean and green energy by then, such as wind turbines and solar panels. More hydroelectric plants and geothermal farms are being neglected because the major oil companies, expanding rapidly, are discouraging the pursuit of renewable energy, making oil cheaper with pipelines.

How We Can Fix This Mess

If you invest in stocks or other securities, instead of investing in pipelines, invest in renewable energy. Our future depends on renewable energy sources as oil and fossil fuels will run out real soon. If the amount of money spent on pipelines everyday is redirected towards environmental conservation, we’d be able to save all the dying ecosystems that pipelines destroy. These aspects of nature are a crucial and proud aspect of Canada’s natural heritage; a loss of such a biodiverse ecosystem cannot be reversed.

What YOU Can Do to Help

  1. Sign our “take action” petition to make your voice heard. This will take us one step closer to our ultimate goal: to stop the expansion of oil pipelines and to save both the environment and our future collectively as humanity.
  2. Let everyone around you know about this important issue. Inform them about the consequences of oil pipeline construction. This is an issue that affects all of us, and we should all be aware of it. Empower them to sign the petition too! The more people, the better!
  3. Finally, take your passion about this issue to one step further. There are many opportunities to help the environment in your daily life. Use less plastic products, waste less resources, and give your support to companies that make a positive impact on our future. Try staying away from using too much electricity wastefully, and get a car that doesn’t run on oil!
